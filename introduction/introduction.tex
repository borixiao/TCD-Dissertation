\chapter{Introduction}
\section{Research Background}
Artificial intelligence has become increasingly visible in marketing practices, including personalised advertising, recommendation systems, customer service chatbots, predictive analytics, and AI-generated content. With the development of generative AI, marketing communication is no longer limited to human-created messages. Brands can now use AI to produce text, images, product descriptions, social media posts, and interactive customer experiences.

At the same time, consumer perception becomes an important issue. Consumers do not only evaluate the functional quality of AI-driven marketing. They may also judge whether AI-generated communication feels trustworthy, authentic, useful, intrusive, or credible. These perceptions can affect how consumers respond to brands, advertising, and purchase decisions.

\section{Research Problem}
Although AI can improve marketing efficiency and personalisation, its influence on consumer perception remains complex. Some consumers may value convenience and relevance, while others may feel uncomfortable with AI-generated content, data tracking, or automated communication. Existing research discusses themes such as trust, authenticity, privacy concerns, perceived usefulness, and purchase intention, but these themes are often examined separately.

This dissertation therefore addresses the need to synthesise existing academic literature and identify the main patterns in how AI and generative AI influence consumer perception in marketing.

\section{Research Aim}
The aim of this dissertation is to examine how artificial intelligence and generative AI influence consumer perception in marketing contexts.

\section{Research Objectives}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item To review academic literature on AI and generative AI in marketing.
    \item To identify key consumer perception themes related to AI-driven marketing.
    \item To analyse how themes such as trust, authenticity, personalisation, privacy concerns, credibility, and purchase intention appear across existing studies.
    \item To develop a thematic understanding of the opportunities and challenges of AI marketing from the consumer perspective.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Research Questions}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item How does existing literature describe the impact of AI and generative AI on consumer perception in marketing?
    \item What recurring themes appear in consumer responses to AI-driven marketing practices?
    \item How do trust, authenticity, personalisation, privacy concerns, and perceived usefulness influence consumer attitudes toward AI marketing?
\end{enumerate}

\section{Scope of the Study}
This dissertation focuses on consumer perception in marketing contexts. It includes studies on AI advertising, AI-generated content, personalised advertising, recommendation systems, chatbots, and social media marketing. It does not aim to evaluate the technical performance of AI systems. Instead, it focuses on how consumers interpret and respond to AI-mediated marketing communication.

\section{Dissertation Structure}
Chapter 1 introduces the research background, research problem, aim, objectives, and research questions. Chapter 2 reviews the literature on AI in marketing and consumer perception. Chapter 3 explains the methodology and thematic coding process. Chapter 4 presents the findings from the literature-based thematic analysis. Chapter 5 discusses the implications of the findings. Chapter 6 concludes the dissertation and suggests future research directions.
