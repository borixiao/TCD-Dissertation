\chapter{Findings and Discussion}

\section{Introduction}

This chapter presents the findings from the thematic analysis of the selected literature. It identifies key themes related to consumer perception in AI-driven marketing and discusses how these themes interact.

\section{Overview of Identified Themes}

The analysis revealed five main themes:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Trust
    \item Authenticity
    \item Personalisation
    \item Privacy Concerns
    \item Purchase Intention
\end{itemize}

These themes represent the most common patterns found across the selected studies.

\section{Trust}

Trust is one of the most frequently discussed themes in AI marketing literature. Many studies suggest that AI can both increase and decrease consumer trust.

On one hand, AI systems are often perceived as efficient and data-driven, which can enhance trust. On the other hand, the lack of transparency and human involvement may reduce trust, especially when consumers are aware that content is generated by AI.

\section{Authenticity}

Authenticity is closely related to how consumers evaluate AI-generated content. Several studies indicate that AI-generated messages may be perceived as less authentic, particularly in emotional or human-centred communication.

This effect becomes stronger when consumers are informed that the content is created by AI. As a result, authenticity plays a key role in shaping consumer attitudes.

\section{Personalisation}

AI enables a high level of personalisation in marketing. Personalised recommendations and targeted advertising can improve relevance and user experience.

However, excessive personalisation may also lead to discomfort, especially when consumers feel that their data is being overly monitored.

\section{Privacy Concerns}

Privacy concerns are a major issue in AI-driven marketing. Consumers may worry about how their personal data is collected and used.

These concerns can negatively influence trust and reduce the effectiveness of AI-based marketing strategies.

\section{Purchase Intention}

AI influences consumer decision-making through recommendation systems and targeted communication.

When consumers perceive AI as useful and trustworthy, purchase intention tends to increase. However, negative perceptions related to authenticity or privacy may reduce willingness to purchase.

\section{Interrelationship Between Themes}

The findings suggest that these themes are interconnected. For example:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Lower authenticity can reduce trust
    \item Privacy concerns can weaken the positive effects of personalisation
    \item Trust plays a central role in determining purchase intention
\end{itemize}

This indicates that consumer perception in AI marketing is not influenced by a single factor, but by the interaction of multiple themes.

\section{Discussion}

The findings align with existing literature that highlights both the benefits and risks of AI in marketing. While AI improves efficiency and personalisation, it also introduces challenges related to trust, authenticity, and privacy.

This supports the idea that AI marketing involves a trade-off. Consumers may accept AI-driven services if the perceived benefits outweigh the risks.

\section{Summary}

This chapter has presented the main findings from the thematic analysis. It has shown that consumer perception is shaped by multiple interconnected factors. The next chapter will conclude the study and discuss implications and future research.
